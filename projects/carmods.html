Carmods

Innovative and competitive

    Carmods is a project made for the lecture of Applied Eletronics, lecture that the professors guides a Completion of Course Work, for the Technical Course in Electronics Integrated to the High School of UTFPR.

    The intent was to design a product that could be introduced to the market. Thinking about it, it was created the Tilt-Down Module of Carmods, in a nutshell it can be define as:

   A ‘Plug and play’ module that implement the vehicle the function to automatically tilt the eletric mirrors when the reverse is active, facilitating parallel park and others parking maneuvers. Offering the following modes of use: Automatic, Semi-automatic and a new third one, the dynamic.

    The project, adds a new function, not yet on the market, drawing attention for the use of professional methos like weld in JLCPCB cards and by the success in implementing the prototype in a car. Reaching the maximum grade rated by Professor Rubens Alexandre de Faria.
